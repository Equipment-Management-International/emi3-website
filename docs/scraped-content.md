## 11. Sensory Based Data (/sensory-based-data)

### Hero Section
**Heading:** Sensory Based Data
**Tagline:** the missing link

### The Evolution of Maintenance Practices
Discusses the historical shift from reactive to preventative maintenance (PM), noting that decades ago, mining maintenance was reactive — fixing equipment only after it broke. The introduction of preventative maintenance and sensor technologies improved productivity, though gaps in defect reporting persist.

### The Persistent Problem
- Paper-based service sheets and defect notes often get lost in filing cabinets or go unrecorded in the CMMS
- Missed defect reporting occurs when field technicians lack time or tools

### Bridging the Gap: The Missing Link
Core concept: A system integrating sensory based data — what technicians see, hear, smell, and touch — with the sensor-based data collected automatically.

### Why Sensory-Based Data Matters
Field technicians detect failure signs including:
- Unusual noises indicating bearing wear
- Subtle vibrations pointing to misalignment
- Odors signaling overheating or contamination

### The Solution: Digital Tools for Real-Time Integration
Three-step process:
1. **In-field Data Collection:** Technicians use mobile devices to record observations during inspections
2. **Integrated Defect Reporting:** Observations are automatically logged into the CMMS
3. **Correlated Data Analysis:** Combining sensory and sensor data provides a comprehensive view

### The Benefits of a Unified System
- Identify hidden defects before they escalate
- Improve decision-making with a complete asset health picture
- Reduce downtime and maintenance costs

### Proven Tools for the Mining Industry
EMI software solutions:
- ZoneMaintenance (mobile equipment)
- RouteMaintenance (fixed plants)
- ComponentStatus (individual components)
- SafetyStatus (safety audits/inspections)
- EnviroStatus (environmental assessments)

### Benefits of an integrated system for a complete picture
When all these pieces of information are aligned with other sources, the picture is full and clear, which means more effective decisions can be made.
